# What a gold answer is — PRINCIPLES + how to derive THIS question's bar

Never a template: analysts ask too many different questions for any type
catalog to hold. Derive the bar fresh, per question, from these principles:

1. QUANTIFY AND RECONCILE — state the headline number(s) and make the parts
   visibly add to the net; an offset has a negative share and is said so.
2. DATE WHAT HAPPENED — when time is involved, the path matters: dated jumps,
   phases, not just start/end difference.
3. DECOMPOSE THE WHY, as far as the data allows — which dimension drove it,
   and what KIND of change it was (new positions vs expiries vs revaluation of
   existing ones). Prefer MRX-native explain data when it exists.
4. STATE CONCENTRATION when it is the story — one name/tenor vs broad-based;
   offsetting round-trips called out.
5. CONTEXT, honestly — units as reported, the COB window used, limit headroom
   when limits are known (omit limit talk when they aren't), standard
   instrument knowledge clearly LABELLED as context (a managed-band pair's
   vega = band-stress exposure), never asserted as the cause.
6. CLAIM NOTHING THE NUMBERS DON'T SUPPORT — no invented market stories;
   derivable structure (paired legs, new-position builds) is required, not
   optional.
7. MATCH SIZE TO THE QUESTION — a lookup: two sentences + the number; a full
   variation analysis: a sectioned note of 3-4 sections, each a few sentences.
   A note that could be a third shorter without losing a decision-relevant
   fact is too long. Never pad; window/units context is a line, not a section.

## Deriving the bar (the Designer's method)
From the target, ask: does time matter (→ principle 2 sections)? does "why"
matter (→ 3)? is concentration plausibly the story (→ 4)? what magnitude
context exists (→ 5)? Then design ONLY the sections those answers earn — typically 2-4; fold the
data-context/window into the summary rather than a standalone section — each
with what it must establish and the data that feeds it. The blueprint IS this
question's contract.
